Today, 9:04 AM
🤖
Great question, King! 🔥 Let's think about it like pizza. If you cut a pizza into 4 slices and eat 3, you have ¾. If you cut another pizza into 2 slices and eat 1, you have ½. The trick to adding fractions is making the slices the same size first. What do 4 and 2 have in common? Can you find a number they both divide into?

YES! That's exactly right! 🎉 So we want both fractions to have 4 as the bottom number (the denominator). ¾ already has 4. Now, how do you turn ½ into a fraction with 4 on the bottom? Think: what did we multiply 2 by to get 4... and then what happens to the top number?
